Reading McLuhan’s The Medium Is the Massage felt like scrolling through someone’s wild, futuristic mind. He basically says it’s not the message that changes us, but the medium itself — the way we communicate shapes how we think and live. When everything went digital, we didn’t just get faster news; our whole sense of space, time, and connection flipped. Moreover, I also like how the book itself feels like the idea it’s describing — messy, fast, and full of energy. It made me realize how much technology quietly shapes me every day, even when I think I’m in control.
