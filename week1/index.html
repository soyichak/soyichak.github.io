Reflection on "As We May Think"

This is the reflection after reading "As We May Think" by Vannev Ar Bush

  1. Historical Significance: Bush wrote just after WWII, when science had been used for destruction. He urged scientists to turn their talents toward peace by making knowledge more accessible. His essay became a landmark call to rethink the role of science in society.
  2. Analysis: Bush recognized the paradox of progress: more knowledge can also mean more confusion, but instead of slowing research, he argued for better tools to support thinking and memory. In doing so, he treated knowledge not as a static archive but as a living network, where meaning emerges through the connections we create.
  3. Reflection: Bush was optimistic that better access to knowledge would elevate humanity, but he overlooked problems like overload, misinformation, and ethical control.
